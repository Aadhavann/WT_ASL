What is American Sign Language?

American Sign Language (ASL) is a visual language. With signing, the brain processes linguistic information through the eyes. The shape, placement, and movement of the hands, as well as facial expressions and body movements, all play important parts in conveying information.


When was it created?

American Sign Language first appeared in the 1800's with the founding of the first successful American School for the Deaf by Thomas Hopkins Gallaudet and Laurent Clerc (first Deaf Teacher from France) in 1817. With strong roots in French Sign Language, ASL evolved to incorporate the signs students would use in less formal settings such as in their home or within the deaf community.
